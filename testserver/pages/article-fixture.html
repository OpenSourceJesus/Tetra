Lenna

Lenna is a standard test image used in digital image processing, starting in 1973.

History

Before Lenna, the first use of a magazine image to illustrate image processing algorithms was in 1961.

The engineers wanted something glossy to ensure good output dynamic range.

The image spread to over 100 different domains during the 1990s.

It became one of the most used images in computer history.

Criticism

Starting in the mid-2010s, many journals have deemed it inappropriate.

Some institutions have banned the image from publication outright.

Alternative test images are often recommended instead.
